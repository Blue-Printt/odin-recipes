Pasta

Description:

This pasta is a classic, comforting dish featuring perfectly cooked pasta tossed in a rich, savory sauce made from tomatoes, olive oil, garlic, and herbs.

Ingredients:

  • Pasta: 100g (about 3.5 oz) per person. Choose your favorite shape (e.g., spaghetti, penne, or macaroni).
  • Water: About 2.5 to 3 liters (or 6 quarts).
  • Salt: 1 to 2 tablespoons per 450g of pasta. Tip: Your pasta water should taste slightly salty, like the ocean.
  • Olive Oil: 1 tablespoon (used after cooking to prevent sticking, or for the sauce).
  • Tomatoes: 400g of canned crushed tomatoes or fresh, blended tomatoes.
  • Aromatics: 1 diced onion and 2 minced garlic cloves.
  • Herbs & Spices: Salt, black pepper, and dried oregano or fresh basil.
  • Cheese: Grated Parmesan cheese for serving.

Steps to Cook:

  1. Boil Water: Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a vigorous rolling boil over high heat.
  2. Add Salt: Once boiling, generously season the water with salt.
  3. Add Pasta: Submerge the pasta in the water. If using long pasta like spaghetti, push it down gently as it softens.
  4. Stir: Stir immediately for the first minute to prevent the noodles from sticking to the bottom or clumping together.
  5. Test for Doneness: Check the package instructions for cooking time. About 1 minute before the time is up, taste a noodle. It should be cooked through but slightly firm in the center (al dente).
  6. Reserve Water: Before draining, scoop out about 1/2 cup of the starchy pasta water.
  7. Drain: Pour the pasta into a strainer. Do not rinse it, as the starch helps the sauce stick to the noodles.
  8. Heat your sauce in a large skillet over medium heat.
  9. Transfer your drained pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce.
  10. Add a splash of the reserved pasta water and toss everything together. The starch in the water combines with the sauce, creating a glossy, rich coating rather than a watery puddle.
  11. Cook together for 1-2 minutes, then serve hot with grated cheese.
