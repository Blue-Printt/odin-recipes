Fried Rice

Description:

Fried rice is a versatile, savory dish made by stir-frying cooked rice in a wok or pan with oil, vegetables, proteins (like chicken, shrimp, or beef), and aromatics.

Ingredients:

  • Rice: 4 cups long-grain parboiled or Basmati rice
  • Broth: 2 - 2.5 cups rich chicken, turkey, or beef stoc
  • 3 cups carrots, diced
  • 2 cups green beans or green peas
  • 1 cup sweet corn
  • 1 cup green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 bunch spring onions (scallions), chopped
  • 2 medium onions, diced
  • 200g-300g cooked and fried cow liver, diced
  • Diced chicken, turkey, or prawns (seasoned and fried)
  • 2 tbsp curry powder and 1 tbsp thyme
  • 2-3 seasoning cubes (e.g., Knorr)
  • Minced garlic and ginger (to taste)
  • Vegetable oil, butter, and salt to taste

Steps to Cook:

  1. Wash your chicken or turkey and season with chopped onions, garlic, ginger, curry, thyme, and seasoning cubes. Steam for 10-15 minutes, add water, and cook until done.
  2. Take out the meat and fry or grill it until crisp. Strain and save the rich broth.
  3. Season the liver with salt and spices, boil, dice, and fry it separately.
  4. Wash the raw rice thoroughly with hot water and salt until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and keeps the grains separate.
  5. Heat a pot with a little vegetable oil and a dash of butter. Add a little curry, thyme, and chopped onions, then add the washed, drained rice.
  6. Toast-fry the rice on medium heat for 10-15 minutes until the grains are fragrant and opaque.
  7. Pour in the reserved meat stock and a little water (just enough to sit slightly below the level of the rice). Cover and cook on medium heat until the rice is about 80-90% done.
  8. In a large wok or separate frying pan, heat oil and sauté diced onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  9. Add the harder vegetables (like carrots and green beans) and fry for 4-5 minutes.
  10. Add the sweet corn, green peppers, fried liver, and proteins. Season with a small amount of curry, thyme, and white or black pepper to taste. Fry on medium-high heat until they are tender yet crunchy. (Avoid overcooking).
  11. Add the partially cooked rice into the pot/wok of vegetables in batches.
  12. Stir-fry continuously on medium-high heat for about 5 minutes until all ingredients are evenly mixed and the flavors meld.
  13. Fold in the spring onions, let it simmer for 2-3 minutes, and remove from heat.
